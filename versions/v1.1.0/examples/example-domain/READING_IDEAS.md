> **Example domain file.** Fictional content showing the ideas/parking-lot pattern. This file is read on demand, never at session startup. It holds future reading and tooling ideas so they're captured without cluttering the active QUEUE. An idea here is a "maybe someday", not a commitment.

## Tooling / process ideas (not committed)

- **Spaced-repetition for book notes.** Pull the 4-and-5-star notes into a flashcard rhythm so the ideas actually stick. Idea only - would need a tool and a habit, both of which are real costs. Park until the notes leaf has enough entries to be worth it.
- **Monthly shelf review.** Once a month, re-read the one-line verdicts on the shelf. Catches books that seemed great at the time but didn't leave a trace - useful signal about what reading actually changes vs. what just feels productive.
- **A "did not finish" section on the shelf.** Right now an abandoned book just vanishes. Recording WHY it was abandoned (boring? wrong time? badly written?) might be more useful than pretending it never happened.

## Open questions

- Is parallel reading actually helping, or is it just two slower books? Worth tracking finish-rate for a couple of months before deciding.
- Should articles get their own shelf, or is the keep/read/cull triage enough? Leaning toward "triage is enough" - articles rarely deserve long memory.
